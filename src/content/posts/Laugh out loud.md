>*A middle-aged man was feeling very down. Everything **had been going** wrong for him. He had problems at work and his wife had left him for another man, after he **had been feeling** depressed for over a month, he decided to go to the doctor. He had to wait for what seemed like ages in the doctor's surgery. The bleeding man next to him was looking at his watch nervously; a woman was coughing and sneezing badly; and a baby was screaming. Finally after he **had been waiting** for about half an hour, he was called in for his consultation.*

## Lead in
picture (a man, in hospital, depressed)
What can we know from this picture?
What else do you wanna know?
Why he looks upset?Why he go to the doctor?
Let's dive into the story and identify more
## Presentation
Tense: past tense
Mark out structure: **had + been v-ing**
- Everything **had been going** wrong for him. 
（before he felt depressed）
- ..., after he **had been feeling** depressed for ==over a month==,...
(before he went to the doctor)
- Finally after he **had been waiting** for ==about half an hour==,...
(before he was called into surgery)
Past perfect continuous tense
To emphasis activity or status was happening in the past. It's continuous.
